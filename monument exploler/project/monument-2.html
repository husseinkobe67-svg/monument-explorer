According to the famous legend of Kız Kulesi, an emperor was told by a fortune teller that his daughter would die from a snake bite on her eighteenth birthday. To protect her, the emperor built a tower in the middle of the sea and sent his daughter to live there. However, on her birthday, a basket of fruit was brought to the tower, and a snake hidden inside the basket bit the princess, causing her death. Because of this tragic story, Kız Kulesi is known as the Maiden’s Tower and is seen as a symbol of fate and destiny in Turkish legends.

Facts

  • Location: Istanbul, Turkey
  • First Built: Around 410 BC
  • Function: Lighthouse, defense tower, and landmark
